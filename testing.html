Summary

There was a lack of testing and treatment of the water in the greater Flint area which caused huge health and water issues, and impacted the health of most residents of all ages. The waterway consisted of raw sewage from the city’s waste treatment plant, which also contributed to the high lead and general contaminant levels in the drinking water. This water was by no means safe for drinking, but was still given to the community without any forms of accurate and deep testing. This was extremely irresponsible of the government officials of Flint as it was shown that they are willing to distribute contaminated and untested water to their residents.

A major issue of the Flint Water was the concerning amount of lead and copper within the community drinking water. This caused major health issues for all demographics within the community, especially those of a younger age. Due to being introduced to concerning levels of contaminated water at such a young age, most of these kids will have health issues for a majority of their lifetime. This sets back children academically and will link them with various learning disabilities and physical illnesses, affecting them in future aspirations as well. Overall, switching the root of the drinking water to the Flint River in order to save money has caused the community to gain lifelong health and trust issues that most if not all of the members of the community had no say over.

Lack of Testing

The calculated amount of children who exceeded the CDC’s guidelines in regards to toxicated drinking water affected expected lifetime earnings and general behavior. Logistic regression and least squares models were used to model and collect the exposure to lead levels in the neighborhood's soil as well as the hazardous contaminants being fed into the water system, all factors of the risen blood lead levels in the children. The data conducted that children and pregnant women were at most risk from drinking the contaminated water, as lead levels were more predominant in those areas of people. On top of that, most individuals who fell into this category reside below the poverty level in residential areas and simply do not have access to clean and safe drinking water. So, they fully relied on the water filtered and distributed from the Flint river. While saving money can be seen as important in regards to balancing a city's finances, there was a reason switching from the Detroit water system to the Flint river water was noticeably cheaper. When people's health are on the line, an intense line of testing should have been conducted and supervised before releasing this drinking water to the public.



There were many warnings and concerns that were voiced in regards to the use of the Flint River as a main source of drinking water for the greater population of the community. In fact, many officials such as Mike Glasnow, who is a laboratory and water quality supervisor for the Flint water plant, stated that he did not ever give the “OK” of sending the water out anytime soon for community drinking water as nowhere than enough testing has been done to ensure the safety and drinkability of the water. He also noted that if the water were to be distributed early, as it was, that it would be directly against his will and direction. Within a few weeks of this new switch in drinking water, residents claimed that there was an odd taste, color, and odor to the water which caused them to have some very strange and poor side effects. After all of these complaints, the city of Flint was finally required to by the SDWA Lead and Copper Rule to conduct extensive sampling for lead and copper, in which the city revealed the lived in the 90th percentile, in which the lead and copper level in the water was 3 three times higher than the previous 15 years. However, it was too late as a good amount of the community had already been negatively impacted by the toxic drinking water the city of Flint passed out for distribution.


Effects

An estimated 140,000 individuals were exposed to lead and other deadly and toxic contaminants due to the community wide drinking water from the Flint River. Most children exposed to the water from the Flint River obtained lead poisoning and childhood exposure to lead and copper can lead to major damage in the brain and nervous system. This can cause apparent learning and behavioral problems as well as speech and hearing problems, overall contributing to a slowed growth and development. This obviously puts these children at an extreme disadvantage to other children in other parts of the country, since other children are most likely not obtaining the learning and behavioral problems these Flint residents are obtaining as well as that they most likely are not living in poverty as well. Unfortunately, most of the children sampled and exposed to these deadly contaminants were toddlers and of the age of 2-5. It is even studied that there is no safe level of lead exposure in children as any level of lead exposure on children can lead to current and future health issues. In fact, the levels of lead in the Flint river and exposed to these children will most likely have fatal effects on these innocent kids and will unfortunately affect them for a majority of their lives. These children were seen to have lower test scores than other children in different areas who were not exposed to contaminated water.


Even adults who have been exposed to the highly contaminated water produced from the Flint River have seen brutal effects. Adults that have been exposed to the lead can greatly increase risk for kidney disease, heart disease and high blood pressure. It also in some cases has reduced fertility as well. Furthermore, adults exposed to the Flint river water experienced hair loss, skin rashes. These were directly correlated to the heavy metals in the drinking water. Rather than physical, a good amount of adults also reported signs of depression and anxiety, as well as PTSD symptoms directly correlated to the Flint Water Crisis. These adult residents exposed to the tap water also experienced increased nausea and emotional agitation, directly pointing towards heightened psychological symptoms. So, it was clearly shown that the introduction of the contaminated water affected adults both physically and mentally. In fact, according to a Cornell study, 26.3% of the Flint residents experienced and exhibited depressive or anxious symptoms and 29% of residents met the criteria for trauma from the contaminated water. The high levels of contaminants and bacteria in the drinking water has killed dozens of people and left children ill. In fact, it has gotten to such a point where there has been a city-declared state of emergency until this situation is taken care of. If more testing had been conducted before the release of the Flint River for community use, most of this situation would have been prevented. Regardless, Flint’s adult residents had increased levels of negative health symptoms during the years of the prime of the water crisis as well as the years after than what should be expected of city population size like Flint.
