Summary

There was a lack in testing and treatment of the water in the greater Flint area which caused huge health and water issues, and impacted the health of most residents. The waterway consisted of raw sewage from the city’s waste treatment plant, which also contributed to the high lead levels in their drinking water.

A major issue of the Flint Water was the concerning amount of lead and copper within the community drinking water. This caused major health issues for all demographics within the community, especially those of a younger age. Due to being introduced to concerning levels of contaminated water at such a young age, most of these kids will have health issues for a majority of their lifetime. Overall, switching the root of the drinking water to the Flint River in order to save money has caused the community to gain lifelong health issues.

Lack of Testing

The calculated amount of children who exceeded the CDC’s guidelines in regards to toxicated drinking water affected expected lifetime earnings and general behavior. Logistic regression and least squares models were used to model and collect the exposure to lead levels in the neighborhood's soil as well as the hazardous contaminants being fed into the water system, all factors of the risen blood lead levels in the children.



There were many warnings and concerns that were voiced in regards to the use of the Flint River as a main source of drinking water for the greater population of the community. In fact, many officials such as Mike Glasnow, who is a laboratory and water quality supervisor for the Flint water plant, stated that he did not ever give the “OK” of sending the water out anytime soon for community drinking water as nowhere than enough testing has been done to ensure the safety and drinkability of the water. He also noted that if the water were to be distributed early, as it was, that it would be directly against his will and direction. Within a few weeks of this new switch in drinking water, residents claimed that there was an odd taste, color, and odor to the water which caused them to have some very strange and poor side effects. After all of these complaints, the city of Flint was finally required to by the SDWA Lead and Copper Rule to conduct extensive sampling for lead and copper, in which the city revealed the lived in the 90th percentile, in which the lead and copper level in the water was 3 three times higher than the previous 15 years.


Effects

An estimated 140,000 individuals were exposed to lead and other deadly and toxic contaminants due to the community wide drinking water from the Flint River. Most children exposed to the water from the Flint River obtained lead poisoning and childhood exposure to lead and copper can lead to major damage in the brain and nervous system. This can cause apparent learning and behavioral problems as well as speech and hearing problems, overall contributing to a slowed growth and development. It is even studied that there is no safe level of lead exposure in children as any level of lead exposure on children can lead to current and future health issues.


Even adults who have been exposed to the highly contaminated water produced from the Flint River have seen brutal effects. Adults that have been exposed to the lead can greatly increase risk for kidney disease, heart disease and high blood pressure. It also in some cases has reduced fertility as well. The high levels of contaminants and bacteria in the drinking water has killed dozens of people and left children ill. In fact, it has gotten to such a point where there has been a city-declared state of emergency until this situation is taken care of. If more testing had been conducted before the release of the Flint River for community use, most of this situation would have been prevented.
